THE PROBLEM WITH GOOGLE

Google started with a noble mission: "to organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful." But over time, its focus shifted to data collection and targeted advertising.

Today, Google tracks your every move online and offline. From search to email, maps to documents, Google's services are designed to collect as much data about you as possible.

This data is used to build detailed profiles of users, which are then sold to advertisers. Even if you don't click on ads, your data is still valuable to Google and its partners.

It's time to break free from Google's surveillance and take control of your digital life.
